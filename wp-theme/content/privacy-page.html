The short version

We collect the minimum data needed to send you the TechRadar PDF, follow up on questions, and run a fast, anonymous website. We don’t sell anything to advertisers and we don’t profile you. You can ask us to delete everything at any time: see section 09.

Section 01

Who we are

The Test Automation TechRadar is a joint initiative of three Dutch test-automation specialists, collectively referred to as "TAG" (Test Automation Group). When you submit a form on this site, your data is shared between the partner most relevant to your request and a shared inbox managed by the group.

  • deTesters B.V.
  • TestCoders B.V.
  • TechChamps B.V.

All three companies are registered in the Netherlands and act as joint data controllers under Article 26 GDPR for the purposes described in this policy. For full registration details, see section 12.

Section 02

The data we collect

We only collect information you actively give us, plus a small amount of technical data needed to run the website. We never buy or enrich contact lists.

We do not collect special-category data (health, political opinions, biometrics), nor do we knowingly process data from anyone under 16.

Section 03

How we use it

Each form has a specific purpose, and we only use your data for that purpose plus the closely related follow-up.

Get the TechRadar PDF

  • Deliver the PDF to your inbox, and notify you about new editions (max twice a year).
  • Understand who reads the radar in aggregate, never tied back to an individual.

Working session requests

  • Match you with the right expert from the three partner companies.
  • Propose times and prepare for the session based on the context you share.

Partner questions

  • Route your message to the specific partner you selected, and only that partner.

Customer-case submissions

  • Plan an interview and co-write the case with you. We always check with you before publishing anything.

Section 04

Legal basis

Under the GDPR, we rely on one of the following grounds for each processing activity:

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): when you tick the consent box to receive the PDF or hear about new editions. You can withdraw this at any time.
  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b)): when we schedule a working session, deliver a paid engagement, or co-produce a case study with you.
  • Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)): for routing partner-question forms, basic anti-spam, and aggregate analytics. We balance this against your reasonable expectations.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): invoicing, tax records, and complying with lawful requests from supervisory authorities.

Section 05

Sharing & recipients

Your data is shared only with the parties needed to run this service. We list them all here, by category:

  • The three partner companies: joint controllers; see section 01.
  • Email infrastructure: a single EU-hosted transactional email provider for sending the PDF and notifications.
  • Form processor: the WordPress plugin Contact Form 7, running on our own EU-based server.
  • Analytics: a privacy-focused, cookieless analytics tool (see section 06).
  • Authorities: only when required by a court order or lawful request.

What we don’t do. We don’t share your data with advertising networks, social platforms, or data brokers. There is no “Sell my data” toggle on this site because there’s nothing to sell.

Section 07

Retention

We keep your data only as long as we have a clear reason to:

  • Newsletter subscribers: until you unsubscribe, after which we retain a hash of your email for 12 months so we don’t re-send by accident.
  • Working-session and partner enquiries: up to 24 months after the last meaningful contact, then anonymized.
  • Customer-case material: kept for the lifetime of the published case; drafts deleted within 6 months if not used.
  • Invoicing records: 7 years, as required by Dutch tax law.
  • Server logs: 14 days, then automatically purged.

Section 08

Security

We follow practices we’d expect from any team we work with: TLS 1.2+ on every endpoint, encryption at rest, role-based access, MFA on all admin accounts, and an annual review of who has access to what. We log access to personal data and review those logs quarterly.

If we ever experience a breach that affects your data, we’ll notify the Dutch supervisory authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens) within 72 hours and contact you directly if there’s a meaningful risk to your rights.

Section 09

Your rights

Under the GDPR you have a set of rights over your personal data. You can exercise any of them by emailing privacy@testautomationtechradar.com. We respond within 30 days.

  • 01Access

    Get a copy of the personal data we hold about you.

  • 02Rectification

    Correct anything that’s inaccurate or incomplete.

  • 03Erasure

    Ask us to delete your data. We comply unless we’re legally required to keep it.

  • 04Restriction

    Pause processing while we look into a question or correction.

  • 05Portability

    Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.

  • 06Objection

    Object to processing based on legitimate interest, including profiling.

  • 07Withdraw consent

    Pull back any consent you gave us, without affecting prior processing.

  • 08Complain

    Lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority at any time.

Section 10

International transfers

Our infrastructure runs in the European Union, and we prefer EU-based vendors. Where a sub-processor is based outside the EU, we rely on the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and carry out a transfer impact assessment before signing.

You can request the current list of sub-processors and the safeguards in place at any time.
